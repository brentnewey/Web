Source:

Brusilovsky, P. et al. (eds.), "Methods and Techniques of Adaptive Hypermedia." Kluwer Academic Publishers, 1998

Summary by Brent Newey

Brusilovsky discusses the requirements, techniques, and applications of Adaptive Hypermedia from the perspective of existing hypermedia applications. The following is a basic outline of the paper:

  • Methods and Techniques of Adaptive Hypermedia
    • A review of where they are used and what specifically is adapted.
    • What the goals of existing systems are and what they should be.
    • What features of the user should be used to perform the adaptation.
    • The two types of adaptive hypermedia: Adaptive Presentation and Adaptive Navigation Support.
    • The main ways in which a website can have its presentation adapted: hiding, annotation, and reordering.
  • In what situations is Hypermedia most useful
    • A discussion on a variety of applications in terms of knowledge level of the user and how these applications present the appropriate knowledge.
    • Page manipulation: fragment variants vs. page variants.
    • Achieving adaptation goals, such as guidance and orientation for the user.
  • User Modeling
    • Specificity of the topic in regards to Adaptive Hypermedia.
    • Methods for collecting data: users vs. program generation.
