Source:

Jameson, A. Designing User-Adaptive Systems. German Research Center for Artificial Intelligence, 2001.

Summary by Brent Newey

This is a tutorial for "Designing User-Adaptive Systems". It includes a deal of preliminary explanation of terms (such as the difference between "adaptibility" and "adaptation"). It also discusses the benefits and drawbacks of adaptation in a general sense, as well as general classifications of the types of users likely to use adaptives systems (skill level, for example).

The tutorial then breaks down a variety of existing models for adaptive websites. The more important examples illustrate the following: Medical Systems, Product Recommendations (Amazon stuff), News Servers (CNN stuff), Online Help (Stupid Microsoft Paper Clip), or general navigation and menu support. It also illustrates a bunch of other adaptive systems of less relevance to Adaptive Hypermedia.

Each example is broken down, explained, and its Adaptive System diagrammed (user metrics->computation->user model->computation->adaptation).
