Source:

Oliver, A., Regragui, O., Monmarché, N., Venturini, G. "Genetic and Interactive Optimization of Web Sites." 2002. Eleventh International World Wide Web Conference. 12 September 2002. <http://www2002.org/CDROM/poster/57/>.

Summary written by Anitra

Optimization of Web sites:

  • Their research was aimed at helping to design a website - "an automatic tool to make... valuable creative suggestions"
  • The user would select members of the current population; the next generation would be the selected members plus offspring, and the process would be repeated.
  • Because it was a recommendation system, they limited their population size to 12, as that was the most that could feasibly be displayed at one time.

Layout optimization

Working with a limited number of objects (text and pictures), they placed objects in random positions within a table. Mutation randomly regenerated the position in the table for one item, while crossover created one child by picking and choosing elements from two parents until all elements were present.
